Part 1 — Understanding the Problem
Before you can improve a product, you need to understand who it's for and what it needs to do. This section sets the foundation for everything that follows.


Who is the user?
Think about who is most likely to download and use Forage, and what their goals are. Be specific — a real person in a real situation, not a category.

→  What are they trying to accomplish?
→  What are their current alternatives to Forage?
→  What frustrates them about those alternatives?
→  Why would someone choose Forage over the alternatives?
→  What context are they in when they first open the app?

What does success look like for the user?
Think about what the user's goals are with Forage — what they need to experience to feel like it's worth their time, and how that might evolve.

→  What does the user need to experience in their first session to feel like Forage is worth their time ("aha moments")?
→  What keeps someone coming back after the first session — and is that different from what got them to try it?
→  What would make someone still be using Forage six months from now — and is that the same reason they downloaded it?


1. Aha moments 
- The first time users actually get a recipe worth trying. 
- Or more meaningfully they actually try that recipe and they actually like it.

2. What brought them back 
Exactly the Aha moments. 

3. Six months goal ≠ original goal? 
After six months user have some experience with the recipes and tried more than a few of them. Their objective at that point of time is not to try this approach, at this time it is their approach and they want to experience more variations of that same.

What is the business trying to drive?
Products have to work for the business too. Think about what Forage needs to achieve commercially, and where that might align — or conflict — with what users want.

→  What does success look like for Forage in the near, medium, and long term?
→  What metric(s) would tell you Forage is healthy at each horizon?
→  How does Forage make money — or how could it?
→  Are there moments where user goals and business goals might pull in different directions?

1. Success criteria
Near - User completes onboarding and tries that recipe.
Medium - User comes the second time for more.
Long term - User becomes a paid member and has tried various recipes and actually liked and recommends the app.
